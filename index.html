Learn chess openings

As you know, if you play chess, you know that everyone is trying to climb the rating ladder. people look for famous chess openings on youtube or they buy courses so that they can master that particular opening and climb the rating ladder. Some of the chess openings that I am going to show you are down -

  1. Sicilian defense. Sicilian defense is a opening which starts with the move e4 and then black responds with c5. It's really good opening for anyone if they know what they're doing.If they have some prep with it then They're fine. You need to have a weapon against this opening. some of the famous variations are alphin variation with the move c3, Smith mora gambit starting with the move d4 allowing black to take the pawn and then offer another pawn on c3 and more.   2. The vienna The vienna starts with the move e4 e5 and then knight to c3 (nc3) There is also a Vienna Gambit in this. when the opponent play the move knight to f6 and then we offer black a pawn. now the best move is to decline with the move pawn to d6. There are some tricky variations in the accepted line when black takes the pawn. there are many variations. check this video to learn all the main variations of the vienna gambit.


  3. Caro Kann caro kann starts with the e4 c6 and then d4 is the most expected move. then we play d5 trying to exchange the pawns. But the most natural move is to push the pawn because that dont allow the normal knight move. now we play c5 and if white takes the pawn then we recapture with the bishop and we have 2 central pawns against of one and we have a piece out before white has. There is a lot to play with this opening. Check out the video below
  4. Queen's gambit Queens gambit starts with the move d4 d5 c4. white offers a pawn to black. The objective is that if black takes, it's known as the queens gambit accepted line. you can take the control of the center and will soon regain that pawn on c4 by e4 with having the threat of bishop takes on c4 if b5 supporting the c4 pawn, you just try to deflect the pawn by the move a4. If black plays a6 thinking that they protected the pawn then they didnt. because if a takes b5 a takes b5 and then the rook hangs. So black plays the move c6 and now you dont take you just maintain the pressure and develop take the center and then castle kingside. it's not obvious that you are going to face only the accepted line. you can face the declined lines too. so prepare for that as well. One of the main weapons against the queens-gambit is the move e5 which is known albin countergambit. This is a very tricky gambit. check out the video down to prepare some of the main lines. Learn endgames

Endgame is a very important part of the game. You need to learn endgames. For example, if you are having a queen and a king and 0 pawns vs a lonely king and 0 pawns but you dont know how to win a endgame with a queen, then its very bad because you are up a queen and the game will end in a draw. So you need to learn endgames.

  1. Queen Endgame The Queen endgame is very easy and simple. Rule no.1 keep knight opposition with the queen. Keep a knight opposition with the queen to the king. Your motive is to bring the king to the corner and then bring your king and then checkmate your opponent. But be careful about one thing, In a position like this image dont play queen to f7! because if you do the game will result in a draw by stalemate. So you have to be careful about this part and then bring your king closer in a position on which the king can get checkmated .Like in this position, g7 looks good so bring your king to g6, and black has to shuffle his king back and forth until checkmate. That was all about the queen end game.
